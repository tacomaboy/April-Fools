Rivian Announces
Full Transition to
Gasoline Power

"A bold new era for adventure," says CEO RJ Scaringe. Effective Q4 2026, all Rivian vehicles will be powered by gasoline engines.

April 1, 2026 | Rivian Automotive, Inc. | Investor Relations
Rivian Press Release — April 1, 2026
Order Now

NORMAL, Illinois — Rivian Automotive, Inc. (NASDAQ: RIVN) today announced a landmark strategic pivot: the company will transition its entire consumer vehicle lineup from battery-electric to gasoline-powered drivetrains, with first deliveries of the new Gas Edition vehicles beginning in Q4 2026.

The announcement, made by Founder and CEO RJ Scaringe at the company's Normal, Illinois manufacturing facility, arrived alongside the unveiling of two new models: the R1T Gas Edition truck and the R1S Gas Edition SUV — each powered by a choice of naturally aspirated V8 or turbocharged V6 engines.

"After years of relentless innovation in the electric space, we've come to a profound realization: gasoline has been here all along, quietly waiting for us to come home." — RJ Scaringe, Founder & CEO, Rivian Automotive

Scaringe cited several factors behind the decision, including "consistent and enthusiastic customer feedback," evolving market dynamics, and a desire to "reduce charger anxiety by making it impossible to charge at all." He also noted that the smell of gasoline "brings people back to simpler times, which resonates with our adventure-first brand ethos."

The New Gas Edition Lineup

The R1T Gas Edition will be available in two configurations: a 5.0L Naturally Aspirated V8 producing 420 horsepower and an estimated 18 MPG highway, and a 3.5L Twin-Turbocharged V6 producing 350 horsepower and a slightly more impressive 21 MPG highway. Both are rear-wheel drive standard, with an optional mechanical 4WD transfer case upgrade Rivian is calling "Posi-Adventure Mode."

The R1S Gas Edition seats seven and is powered by a 4.6L V8 producing 395 horsepower. With an estimated 15 MPG on the highway, Rivian says the R1S Gas Edition is "perfectly suited for adventurers who love stopping." The front trunk — previously used for groceries or camping gear — has been redesigned as the "Gas Tunnel™," which can hold up to four standard 5-gallon fuel canisters for extended backcountry excursions.

The ExxonMobil Partnership

In concert with the Gas Edition launch, Rivian announced a multi-year strategic partnership with ExxonMobil to co-develop the "Rivian Fuel Experience" — a network of 4,000 branded fueling stations across the United States. Beginning Q3 2026, select ExxonMobil locations will feature Rivian-branded pump handles, an adventure-themed canopy design, and exclusive Rivian Snack Packs available for purchase inside.

"ExxonMobil has more locations than there are Superchargers," said one Rivian executive, who asked not to be named. "We saw an opportunity there." ExxonMobil CEO Darren Woods called the partnership "unexpected but welcome," and confirmed that ExxonMobil will not be adding charging infrastructure.

"Range anxiety? We solved it. You can't be anxious about something that's literally everywhere. Gas stations are on every corner. This is freedom." — RJ Scaringe

Impact on Existing Customers

Rivian confirmed that owners of existing electric R1T and R1S vehicles will continue to receive software updates for "at least several months." The company's Rivian Adventure Network (RAN) charging infrastructure will be "maintained indefinitely, or through Q3 2027, whichever comes first."

Customers who have pre-ordered electric vehicles and wish to convert their order to the new Gas Edition will receive a $500 credit toward their first year of ExxonMobil Synergy Premium fuel. Orders that are not converted will be fulfilled in the original electric configuration "in a reasonable timeframe." Rivian did not define "reasonable."

Investor Reaction

Shares of Rivian Automotive (RIVN) reacted immediately to the news, with trading briefly halted on the NASDAQ. Analysts described the announcement as "certainly a decision that was made" and "something we will need time to process." Wedbush Securities analyst Dan Ives issued a one-word note to clients: "...what?"

Rivian did not provide updated financial guidance at this time. The company confirmed that the transition to gasoline manufacturing represents a capital expenditure of "a lot," and said the board approved the initiative unanimously following what sources describe as "a very expensive catered lunch."

About Rivian

Rivian designs, develops, and manufactures category-defining vehicles and technology for people who want to get out and stay out there. Founded in 2009 and headquartered in Irvine, California, Rivian operates a state-of-the-art manufacturing facility in Normal, Illinois. The company's lineup includes the R1T truck, R1S SUV, and commercial delivery vans for Amazon, all currently electric — for now.
